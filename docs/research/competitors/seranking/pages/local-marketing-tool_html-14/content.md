FAQ
How do you perform a local SEO analysis?
To perform a local SEO analysis, research the following: the local search leader, where your website stands in comparison, what your top and direct competitors are doing SEO-wise, and you and your competitors’ GMB profiles. Monitor Maps results and analyze all location-based factors.
Do I need local marketing tools?
If you’re a business owner or local SEO expert, you can benefit tremendously from a reliable local marketing platform. It can help you manage the following: website and local listings performance tracking, local competitor analysis, and optimizing your website for local search. Use these tools to get comprehensive marketing data, improve research, and simplify local results tracking.
What should a local marketing report include?
A comprehensive local search performance report should include data on keyword rankings, customer reviews, and business listings, including GBP data, for your target markets. It’s also recommended to feature data on competitor performance. This makes it possible to identify your competitive advantages and refine your local SEO strategies.
How can I improve my local visibility on Google?
To boost your local visibility on Google, you need to optimize your Google Business Profile with accurate contact details, business hours, high-quality photos, and a keyword-rich description. Plus, use location-specific keywords on your website and create dedicated pages for each service area. Keep your business name, address, and phone number consistent across all local directories and listings. Encourage customers to leave positive reviews and respond to all feedback to build trust. SE Ranking’s Local Marketing Tool can help you with these tasks.
Does this software work with Google Business Profile?
Yes, SE Ranking’s Local Marketing Tool integrates with Google Business Profile, enabling you to manage business information, monitor rankings, and analyze performance directly within the platform. You can also plan, create, and schedule GBP posts from here.
Can I track local rankings by city or ZIP code?
Yes, the Local Rank Tracker enables you to monitor keyword rankings by specifying locations such as cities or ZIP codes, providing accurate local SEO insights.
Can I manage multiple locations with this tool?
Yes, SE Ranking’s Local Marketing Tool supports managing multiple business locations, making it suitable for multi-location businesses and agencies.
Does the tool allow me to manage and reply to customer reviews?
Yes, you can monitor and respond to customer reviews across various platforms, including Google, Tripadvisor, ShowMeLocal, FindOpen, Facebook, Yelp, and more—directly from the tool. You can update their status, filter reviews by rating, source, status, language, or date, and even export them. Analytics, including an overview of your overall review performance, are also available.
How does the software help me optimize my listings in local directories?
The tool helps you optimize your listings by automatically scanning top local directories for your business information, identifying missing or inconsistent NAP details, and suggesting updates to ensure accuracy across all platforms. Consistent, complete listings boost your credibility with search engines and improve your chances of appearing in local search results.
Is there a free trial or demo available?
Yes, SE Ranking offers a free trial of the Local Marketing Tool with a limit of three listings, allowing you to explore its features before committing.
Is the software suitable for SEO agencies or multi-location businesses?
Yes. The software is designed to support both SEO agencies and multi-location businesses. Agencies can manage multiple clients from a single dashboard, while multi-location businesses can track rankings, monitor reviews, and maintain consistent business information across all locations.
What’s the difference between a traditional SEO tool and a local SEO tool?
A traditional SEO tool focuses on improving a website’s overall search visibility. It covers keyword research, backlink analysis, technical audits, and content optimization, often at a national or global level. A local SEO tool, on the other hand, is specialized for boosting visibility in local search results. It focuses on location-specific keyword tracking, Google Business Profile management, local directory listings, review monitoring, and other features designed to help businesses attract nearby customers.
